About me

I am an astronomer (astrophysicist) and currently a Master student in Physics of Data at University of Padova, with a strong interest in observational astrophysics, particularly focused on minor bodies and planetary astrophysics.

My journey as a student (and aspiring researcher) is driven by a deep curiosity in harnessing the power of machine learning techniques in the field of astrophysics. In addition, I have a strong affinity for data science, which complements my analytical skills.

My Bachelor's thesis provided me with a unique opportunity to spend three nights at the Asiago Observatory (INAF/OAPD). During this time, I had the privilege of observing the Dydimos system after the impact event with the DART probe.

In addition to my academic journey, I have been actively involved in an outreach project since high school, keeping me well-informed about the latest developments and discoveries in the world of astrophysics.
